byour mutual friend great expectations is the thirteenth novel by charles dickens and his penultimate completed novel bildungsroman that depicts the personal growth and personal development of an orphan nicknamed mr_pip
this article is about the charles dickens novel cities followed it is dickens second novel after david copperfield to be fully narrated in the first person
the novel was first published as serial in dickens weekly periodical all the year round from december to august in october chapman and hall published the novel in three volumes
the novel is set in kent and london in the early to mid century and contains some of dickens most memorable scenes including the opening in graveyard where the young mr_pip is accosted by the escaped convict abel_magwitch
and has colourful cast of characters who have entered popular culture these include the eccentric miss_havisham the beautiful but cold miss_estella and mrs_joe_gargery the unsophisticated and kind blacksmith
dickens themes include wealth and poverty love and rejection and the eventual triumph of good over evil has been translated into many languages and adapted numerous time into various media
although dickens contemporary thomas carlyle referred to it disparagingly as that mr_pip nonsense he nevertheless reacted to each fresh instalment with roars of laughter
upon its release the novel received near universal acclaim later george bernard shaw praised the novel as all of one piece and consistently truthful
during the serial publication dickens was pleased with public response to great expectations and its sales when the plot first formed in his mind he called it a very fine new and grotesque idea
in the century the novel retains good ratings among literary critics and is popular with the british public publication in all the year
